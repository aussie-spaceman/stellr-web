# VOICE.md — Stellr Education

We are a professional organisation running professional events. Teachers derive value from them, parents trust us with their children, and mentors give their time. Our voice is professional — and human.

## Brand voice principles
1. **Treat the audience as capable.** Even 12-year-olds — especially newcomers. Never dumb it down; explain unfamiliar terms in passing.
   _"You will be a successful engineer, and we'll show you the how and the why."_
2. **Add value to inspire.** Our social licence to operate comes from the value we add. Specifics beat aspirations.
   _"'Monthly hands-on workshops' beats 'transform your relationship with science.'"_
3. **Be concise.** Words have meaning and accuracy is key. If a sentence still works with a word removed, remove it.
   _"If we can say something in half the words, why waste our members' time?"_
4. **Fun & engaging.** We're inspiring the next generation of STEM professionals — everything is infused with fun, enjoyment and optimism.
   _"Today is the greatest day of your professional life; tomorrow will be better."_
5. **Professionalism is respect.** Acting professionally is how we show respect for the community's time and trust.
   _"We respect your time and your trust in us — we show it by acting professionally."_

## Tone variations
Hold the voice steady; shift register by context.

| Context | Tone | Example opener |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing pages (homepage, audience landings, Membership) | Confident, you-focused, benefit-led | "Hands-on STEM events, every month, plus a community where asking questions is the point." |
| Member-specific content | Practical, specific about what you get and what's expected | "Catalyst tier is for college students who want to lead. You'll train, mentor newcomers, and build a record." |
| Event narratives | Substantive — what you'll actually learn and do | "In this 90-minute workshop you'll learn a, b and c, and walk away with skills x, y, z." |
| Member-facing announcements | Collegial, "we"-focused — you're part of something bigger | "We've added a new resource library this week. It's open to all members." |
| Transactional emails (confirmations, reminders) | Warm but functional, concise | "You're registered for [Event] on [Date]. Zoom link arrives one hour before. Bring a question." |
| Social — Instagram | Extra emphasis on fun and engaging — leans to school & college student members | — |
| Social — LinkedIn | Extra emphasis on professionalism — leans to adult members (teachers, parents, mentors) | — |

## Audience variations
Same voice, tuned to who's reading.

| Audience | Lean into | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| School students (under 18) | Concrete examples, things you'll actually do, real-world relevance — "you don't need to know any X to start" | Sounding like a school admin; "exciting opportunities"; talking down |
| College students | Time-respect ("a 90-minute workshop, not a semester"), context for adult life, low-pressure framing | Pity ("never too late!"); jargon you'd assume they don't know |
| Educators | Practical value to them and their students, respect for their expertise, time-pressure awareness | Edtech sales-speak; assuming they need convincing to care about students |
| Parents | What their student gets out of it, safety and privacy specifics, reassurance | Hype; vague claims; unfounded statistics |
| Donors / sponsors | Concrete impact data, who you're reaching, why this matters | Vague mission language; "transforming lives" |

## Photography & imagery (quick reference)
- **Created imagery:** realistic hardware plausible today or within ~25 years (no teleportation); always frame against other infrastructure or celestial bodies (never a bare starscape); diagrams show more than a background starscape; astronomical backgrounds may ignore brand-colour matching.
- **Event photography:** large-group shots for scale; backgrounds with student work or the venue; prefer adults interacting with students (cross-generational); the 'action' is design-oriented, not hardware.
- **Tone:** always positive and optimistic — reflect the benefits of humanity expanding into the solar system.
