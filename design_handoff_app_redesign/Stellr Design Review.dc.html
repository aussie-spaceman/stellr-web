Product Design Review

Bringing the Stellr
app to life.

A look-and-feel audit of the member web app — and a concrete direction to make it intuitive, collaborative, and genuinely impressive for the rare-but-important moments students, teachers, and mentors spend in it.

Primary goal
Ease of use — intuitive the moment you return
Then
Collaborative — it should feel like a network
And
Visually appealing — a little wow factor
Where I
started

I reviewed the live app and the stellr-web codebase — the member shell, the Community spaces, the Academy/training, the member directory, chat, and account. The good news: the foundations are solid. The information architecture is thoughtful, the brand assets exist (a strong star mark, a navy-and-gold palette, four custom typefaces), and the feature set is genuinely rich.

The gap is almost entirely in the visual execution of the app itself. The brand lives on the marketing site; the app was built with default styling and never received the brand. That's the opportunity below — and most of it is a focused styling pass, not a rebuild.

The diagnosis

What's holding the look & feel back

Four issues, all observed directly in the code. Each maps to one of your goals.

1

The brand never made it into the app

The brand palette — deep navy #051535, blue #0d439d, gold #dda33b, orange #da6220 — is defined in tailwind.config.ts, but the screens themselves are built almost entirely from default Tailwind grays with stray indigo-500 accents. The signature orange and gold are used essentially nowhere. That's the "monochromatic" feeling — it's literally grayscale plus a borrowed purple.

Seen in: community feed, training, directory, chat bubbles (bg-gray-900), account tabs (indigo-600)
What the app actually uses today
What the brand actually is
2

There's no "home" — you land in a list and stall

After sign-in, members land on Spaces — a flat list of discussion rooms. There's no screen that answers the only question an infrequent visitor has: "what should I do right now?" Your next competition, training that's due, new replies, upcoming sessions — all of it lives in separate sections reachable only through hover dropdowns. Each page is an island with no path to the next. This is the "no flow, no sense of what's on offer" you described.

Everything hidden in 3 dropdowns No "what's next" surface No cross-links between areas No progress or status visible
3

Everything whispers

Headings sit at a flat text-2xl while body copy is mostly text-sm text-gray-500. Two of the four brand typefaces — the display faces that create impact — are never used in-app. Nothing draws the eye, so nothing feels exciting. No "wow."

4

It's a community without faces

The directory, posts, and chat show text names only — no avatars, no schools at a glance, no presence. For a platform whose whole point is connecting students with peers and industry pros, the people are invisible. It reads like an admin table, not a network.

The direction

Three principles to design against

01

Show the next step

Because nobody lives here, every visit must open with "here's what's happening and what to do." Orientation over exploration.

02

Put people in the room

Faces, schools, mentors, presence. Make the network visible so collaboration feels like the default, not a feature.

03

Earn the wow with brand

The wow factor is already paid for — it's the navy, gold, the star mark, and the display type sitting unused. Turn them on.

How to get there

A staged roadmap

Ordered by impact-per-effort. The first stage alone changes the entire feel.

Quick wins~1 week
  • Apply brand colors & the warm canvas app-wide
  • Turn on Norwester / Archivo / Fredoka in headings
  • Color-code the three areas
  • Add member avatars everywhere
Core~2–3 weeks
  • Build the Home / "what's next" screen
  • Persistent sidebar nav
  • Refresh cards: covers, progress, presence
  • Event hub with prep checklist
Delightlater
  • Onboarding tour for first-timers
  • Achievement / streak moments after events
  • Richer profiles & portfolios
  • Subtle motion & empty-state illustration

Everything here is built from assets already in the repo. The biggest lever isn't more design — it's deploying the brand you already paid for, and giving members a place to land. Happy to turn any of these mockups into production-ready components next.
